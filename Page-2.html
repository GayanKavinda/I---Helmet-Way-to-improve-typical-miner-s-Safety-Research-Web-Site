Mining industry is an unsafe and very dangerous occupation for human livelihood. About 15,000 workers die from these causes every year. This is because its employees can experience suffocation, exposure to toxic gases, various earthquakes and rock falls. In the event of such an accident, the control room will not be connected and will face very problematic situations. For these reasons, the people who work there do their jobs regardless of survival. At a time when people are still unable to address this issue due to issues like this, we intend to create a safety helmet to address this issue through this project. For the above reasons, the wireless helmet that is expected to be created creates a network to minimize accidents. The main purpose of our helmet is to check the health status of the workers in the mine as well as the temperature at their location as well as changes in the mine. It also ensures the safety of the employees working in the mine by inspecting and monitoring the place of work. Here, the data obtained by each employee is redirected to a main dashboard, which also facilitates data analysis.

02.   Detection of toxis gases in mines    

According to the research paper Smart Safety Helmet in Coal Mining Using Arduino presented by Professors K. Suriyakrishnaana and R. Arun Gandhib, the helmet they designed consists of two parts. They are the Transmitter Division and the Receiver Division. The transmission section consists of ATmega328 microcontroller, collision sensor, MQ5 gas sensor, humidity sensor. In the receiver section, the ATmega328 buzzer interface with the LCD Display and RF receiver. The transmission section is housed in underground excavation areas.

Research Problem and Gap

Safety helmets are used as a mandatory accessory in everyday mining activities. However, it is clear that Sri Lanka and the developing and developing countries do not pay much attention to helmets. This is because of the fact that mining-related deaths are now very common around the world. In the day-to-day mining industry, its employees usually wear safety helmets. But it turns out that most miners these days do their job without a safety helmet. It poses a serious threat to their lives. This is because of the emergencies that occur in rock mines as well as in earthquakes. In such cases, in the event of a serious accident, immediate medical attention is required. Most likely it will go as far as surgery. In the mining industry, there is a high safety risk. There are incidents like rock fall, head injuries. Therefore, these hazards are directly threatening to the safety of miners . In mines most dangerous event is heavy objects fall to the miner's head. The main thing is miners must wear a helmet to prevent hazards. Thus, the helmet is mandatory. Underground mines are dark. Using the normal safety helmet has a flashlight. But only light is not enough to get the safety of miners. Due to hazards, miners' lives take riskier. In coal mines, the significant emanation is of methane. The methane discharged amid and in the wake of mining operations is called Coal mine methane (CMM). Lately, there have been numerous fatalities in underground coal mine blasts in which methane was a contributing element. Additionally, methane is 21 times more intense for ozone depleting substance outflows. The centralization of methane if went through a range in the vicinity of 5% and 15%, it might prompt blast. This scope of methane is known as the dangerous range. Methane can be touched off effectively in this range with the nearness of a start source to make a vicious methane blast that may spread within the sight of flammable coal clean. Even if a normal helmet is worn, the miner will not receive adequate protection. Sometimes Miners hit the heavy objects like mining objects, hard rock which risks their life. Another factor that affects the miners is the inhalation of hazardous gases that provokes them in danger. In such a situation the miners fail their normal safety helmet to communicate with the outside world . However, the use of existing general safety helmets does not provide adequate protection for miners in mine related accidents. If today's technology is not used, if something goes wrong in the mine, the miner will not be able to find it or save his life. For these reasons, some miners express a fear as well as a reluctance to work in high risk areas of the mine. Today, helmets are manufactured worldwide to provide protection to miners around the world. Looking at the major problems in the mining industry, we observed a set of common difficulties, which are categorized into relevant components and listed below. 

1.   Exposure of miners to toxic gases. 

LPG gas in the currently designed smart safety helmet is made to detect only CO2. But there are more harmful gases. They are Hydrogen and Methane carbon monoxide. Smart safety helmets should be manufactured so that they can be identified.    

2.   Workers injured in mines crash, trapped inside the mine and dying.    
Currently, the smart helmet is not designed to detect the nature of the mine walls. To be a formal smart safty helmet, it must be manufactured to detect cracks and water leaks in the mine. 

3.   Inability to monitor the health status of workers in the mine. 
In the currently manufactured smart helmet, only the miner's temperature is taken as health data. But that is not enough. Factors such as oxygen levels in the body should also be considered. Also seek medical advice in the event of an accident.    

4.   Dust and fire in mines. 
Currently, smart helmets are not only interested in the dust in the mines, but they are also unhealthy for miners. Also, helmets are not manufactured to detect a fire in a mine. The helmet should be made so that dust and flame can be detected.
